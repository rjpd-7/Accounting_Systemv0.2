General Ledger and Trial Balance Basic Information

A General Ledger is a company's main accounting record, consolidating all financial transactions into individual accounts like assets, liabilities, revenues, and expenses. It organizes data from journals and subledgers, providing a summary of debits and credits for each account, which is essential for creating financial statements and making informed business decisions.

A Trial Balance is an internal accounting report that lists the ending balances of all general ledger accounts at a specific point in time. Its primary purpose is to verify the mathematical accuracy of a company's double-entry bookkeeping system.
